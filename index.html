    Beyond the 9 to 5

    Analyzing Work-Life Balance, Commuting, and Job Attrition

    üöÄ Project Overview

    Employee attrition is a critical concern for businesses, and understanding the key drivers behind it can help in improving retention strategies. This project investigates how work-life balance and commuting distance impact employee attrition and how job satisfaction mediates this relationship.

    üîç Key Research Question

    How do work-life balance and commuting distance directly impact employee attrition, and how does job satisfaction mediate this relationship?

    üìå Key Findings

    • Work-life balance has a strong positive effect on job satisfaction, meaning employees with better work-life balance are more satisfied.
    • Commuting distance does not significantly impact job satisfaction, but it increases the likelihood of attrition.
    • Job satisfaction is a protective factor, helping to reduce attrition rates.
    • Bayesian modeling confirms that longer commutes correlate with higher attrition probabilities.

    üõ†Ô∏è Methodology & Techniques

    • Dataset: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
    • Bayesian inference using PyMC
    • Logistic regression for attrition probability
    • Data visualization with Matplotlib & Seaborn

    üìä Modeling Breakdown

    1Ô∏è‚É£ Job Satisfaction Model

    • Work-life balance levels were converted into an index to measure their effect on job satisfaction.
    • A linear model was used to estimate how much work-life balance and commuting distance contribute to job satisfaction.

    2Ô∏è‚É£ Attrition Model

    • A logistic regression model was applied to predict attrition probability based on:
      • Work-life balance
      • Commuting distance
      • Job satisfaction

    üî¨ Results & Interpretation

    üìå Work-Life Balance & Job Satisfaction

    • ‚úîÔ∏è Higher work-life balance leads to improved job satisfaction.
    • ‚úîÔ∏è The relationship between work-life balance and job satisfaction was found to be statistically significant.

    üìå Commuting Distance & Attrition

    • ‚úîÔ∏è Employees with longer commutes are more likely to leave.
    • ‚úîÔ∏è No strong evidence that commuting distance directly affects job satisfaction.

    üìå Job Satisfaction as a Buffer

    • ‚úîÔ∏è Higher job satisfaction reduces the likelihood of attrition, even if commuting distance is high.

    üõ†Ô∏è Tech Stack

    • Python: Data processing & modeling
    • PyMC: Bayesian modeling
    • NumPy & SciPy: Statistical analysis
    • Pandas: Data manipulation
    • Matplotlib & Seaborn: Visualization

    This project highlights the importance of work-life balance in employee retention. While commuting distance alone doesn‚Äôt significantly impact job satisfaction, it does increase attrition risks.
